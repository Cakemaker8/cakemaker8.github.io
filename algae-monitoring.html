Algae Monitoring Project

This is the main page for the Algae Monitoring Project, a senior design project completed by Madeline Potter, Ava Rittle, Luke Lansdown, Dalton Taylor, and Tyler Brewer.

Algae itself is not bad, but if let overgrown, it can lead to major environmental damage. This project seeks to detect the conditions that cause algae to overgrow, and if the conditions are measured to be indicative of a bloom, a warning will be displayed below.


Current algal bloom risk: Low


Data is collected on pH, dissolved oxygen, nitrogen, turbidity, temperature, and light spectrometer. It is collected over a period of 15 minutes every 3 hours. Select the month, day, and data collection number you want to view below.
